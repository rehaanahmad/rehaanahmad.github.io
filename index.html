Rehaan Ahmad

Hey! My name is Rehaan Ahmad. I'm a student at Cupertino High School and a machine learning research intern at INRIA. I typically work on the crossroads between remote sensing (specifically, satellite vegetation analysis) and machine learning. However, I have found myself working on projects as far out as quantum computing--in fact, I'm currently exploring topics within the realm of Universal Gate Quantum Computing. The underlying motivation for a lot of my work stems from the old Ben Franklin axiom "an ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure"--a rule that reigns especially true when it comes to problems such as malware detection and famine prevention.

    Working with Senior Researcher Gerardo Rubino, along with 3 other CS researchers, on a graph neural network (GNN) approach to forecasting vegetation. Unlike existing algorithms, this GNN approach has the potential to forecast specific types of vegetation over larger regions.

Research

Graph Neural Networks (Funded)
  • Designing an approach to embed sparsely distributed soybean crop into graphs, and developing a graph neural network to produce 8 and 16 day forecasts of the soybean.
  • Instead of only examining immediate geographic neighbors of crop fields, a graph neural network allows us to connect fields that have similar soil characteristics although they may have varying geographic distance.
  • Our paper is scheduled to be finished by January 2020.
  • In April, I will be going on a paid-for trip to INRIA's Rennes campus in France to visit the team for two weeks and work on another project.
ConvLSTM (Funded)
  • Developed a ConvLSTM based approach to forecasting NASA MODIS satellite-captured vegetation health imagery.
  • First author of the paper "A Machine-Learning Based ConvLSTM Architecture For NDVI Forecasting" submitted to the International Transactions in Operational Research peer-reviewed journal (h-index: 44).
  • Also currently collaborating with scientists from the GEOGLAM crop monitor (a G20 funded initiative to monitor crops worldwide) to have our algorithm incorporated with their system.
  • This research was funded by the Uruguayan Sectorial Commission of Scientific Research (CSIC).
Quantum Annealing Binary Classifier (Granted D-Wave Computing Time)
  • Developed a modification to the QBoost binary classifier algorithm built for a quantum annealing computer.
  • Inspired by the WannaCry ransomware attacks that affected over 300,000 computers, the algorithm was designed to examine a computer's heap and detect heap-spraying attacks.
  • With the modified QBoost binary classifier, the algorithm was optimized with the D-Wave 2000Q quantum computer.
  • As part of my research, I worked on two proofs that provided theoretical justification for why the modification could handle larger optimization problems.

Autonomous Drones w/ Image Recognition (Unfunded)
  • Designed an algorithm that uses drone imagery of citrus trees in order to count the number of oranges on each tree and determine which of the oranges are not growing nominally.
  • Typically, orange orchard monitoring requires manual labor and costs upwards of $50,000 US.
  • With harmful diseases like citrus greening, finding and containing such oranges is critical to the well-being of an orchard.
  • Utilizing regular drone flights can help mitigate the damage of the citrus greening disease.
